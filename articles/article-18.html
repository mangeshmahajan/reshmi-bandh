Dear Abu
Yashashree
I have known you my whole life. You and Aaji were my two pillars that supported the foundation of my values and beliefs. Because of you, I am who I am today, and I abide by what I believe is right, according to what you have taught me. I look forward to your visits so much. Every time I didn’t understand a math problem, or a science question, you were always there to help me out. At times, I didn’t understand the problem at hand, and all the time, you were there to patiently sit and explain it to me. During my formative years, you always used to tell me to inculcate a practice of writing tables from 1 to 20, and I always used to crib when you told me to do so. Today, I’m glad you did, because if you didn’t, I would have been a step behind all my classmates. You used to make me sit when you and Aaji were doing your daily puja, and again, at first, I didn’t really enjoy it. Today, I’m proud that I know these bhajans and shlokas by heart. Going back to when I was just a little one learning to walk (I obviously do not remember), you played a significant role in teaching me how to walk with a chair. I love and cherish the memories we have made together and look forward to making many more. Stay healthy, happy, and safe, always and forever. Love,
Yashashree
